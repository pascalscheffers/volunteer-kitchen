# Trusted recipe sources

Where to cross-check a recipe before it enters the archive — see the sourcing
rule in [`../CLAUDE.md`](../CLAUDE.md) and the validation workflow in
[`writing-guide.md`](writing-guide.md). The aim: **2+ independent, reputable
sources that agree.**

**Bias toward European sources.** We cook in Denmark and shop in Danish (and
German / French / Dutch). For a European dish, a good local source is usually
more authentic *and* doubles as the local-name reference for the shopping list —
so reach for the regional sources below before the Anglo-American ones. For
something genuinely non-European (hummus, say), the international list still
applies.

This list grows as we vet sources. If one repeatedly proves reliable (or
unreliable), note it here so the next contributor doesn't re-learn it.

## European & regional — prefer these

### Denmark / Nordic

- **Valdemarsro** (valdemarsro.dk) — long-running, widely trusted Danish food
  blog; everyday Danish home cooking and baking, well photographed and tested.
- **Meyers** (meyers.dk) — Claus Meyer, co-founder of the New Nordic movement;
  the benchmark for modern Danish.
- **Karolines Køkken** (Arla) — classic Danish home baking (kanelsnegle, boller,
  cakes). It's a dairy-brand site, but the recipes are solid and traditional.
- **Brødrene Price** (on dr.dk) — the Price brothers' Danish TV cooking; strong
  for classic Danish dishes.
- **Trine Hahnemann** — Danish chef and cookbook author who writes in English —
  the easiest reliable gateway to Scandi cooking if you don't read Danish.

### Netherlands

- **Rutger Bakt** (rutgerbakt.nl) — Rutger van den Broek, *Heel Holland Bakt*
  winner; rigorously tested, beginner-friendly baking. The Dutch baking
  benchmark — recipes that reliably work.
- **Allerhande** (ah.nl/allerhande) — Albert Heijn's recipe platform; free,
  huge, and dependable for everyday Dutch home cooking.
- **Laura's Bakery** (laurasbakery.nl) — popular Dutch baking blog; generally
  dependable, still worth a second source.

### Germany / Austria

- **Plötzblog** (ploetzblog.de) — Lutz Geißler; the German-language authority on
  bread and yeasted dough — scientific, exhaustively tested. First stop for
  anything bread- or dough-based.
- **Dr. Oetker** (oetker.de) — German baking institution; classic, dependable
  cake and baking ratios.
- **Essen & Trinken** (essen-und-trinken.de) — reputable German food magazine.
- **Chefkoch** (chefkoch.de) — huge community aggregator; use with care, prefer
  highly-rated entries and corroborate.

### UK / Ireland

- **Felicity Cloake** — the Guardian "How to make the perfect…" column tests the
  major published versions of a dish against each other and synthesises a best
  one. Ideal for cross-checking a classic, since the comparison is already done.
- **Delia Smith** (deliaonline.com) — exhaustively precise and beginner-proof.
- **Mary Berry & Paul Hollywood** — baking benchmarks (*Bake Off*); reliable for
  cakes and bread.
- **Named UK cooks** — Nigel Slater, Nigella Lawson, Jamie Oliver, Hugh
  Fearnley-Whittingstall (River Cottage); tested and dependable.
- **BBC Good Food** — large tested database; a decent default, still worth a
  second source (see "use with care" below).

## Reliable by default (international)

- **King Arthur Baking** — US, but a top tested baking source; gram weights,
  explained technique, strong for bread and enriched/yeasted dough.
- **Serious Eats** — especially J. Kenji López-Alt and Stella Parks (BraveTart,
  for baking). Technique-first, actually tested, explains the *why*.
- **America's Test Kitchen / Cook's Illustrated** — rigorously tested and
  ratio-driven (partly paywalled).
- **Yotam Ottolenghi** (ottolenghi.co.uk, the Guardian column, the books) —
  strong for Levantine, Middle Eastern, and vegetable cooking.
- **Michael Solomonov / Zahav** — the benchmark for hummus and modern Israeli.
- **Named cookbook authors and chefs** generally — a real person's tested
  recipe beats an anonymous page.

## Use with care (cross-check, don't trust alone)

- **Big aggregators** (Chefkoch, AllRecipes, Food Network, and the like) —
  quality varies wildly; prefer highly-reviewed or named-chef entries, and
  corroborate.
- **National food institutions** (BBC Good Food and regional equivalents) —
  generally decent, still worth a second source.
- **Supermarket & brand sites** (Allerhande, Karolines Køkken/Arla, Dr. Oetker)
  — usually well-tested and a fine local reference, but they exist to sell a
  product, so sanity-check the odd ingredient nudge.

## Avoid as a sole source

- **SEO content-farm recipe blogs** — the 2,000-words-of-story-then-a-recipe
  sites built for ad revenue. Often untested, copied, or AI-spun.
- **Anything you can't attribute** to a real person or a tested kitchen.
- **An AI's recollection.** That's the draft, not the source.

## By cuisine (grows over time)

- **Danish / Nordic baking** (kanelsnegle, boller, rye): Valdemarsro, Karolines
  Køkken, Meyers; Plötzblog for the dough science.
- **Dutch baking:** Rutger Bakt, Laura's Bakery.
- **Bread & enriched dough generally:** Plötzblog, King Arthur, Rutger Bakt.
- **Levantine / Middle Eastern:** Ottolenghi, Solomonov/Zahav.
