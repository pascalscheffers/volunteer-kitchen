# Roasted chickpeas

<!-- <img src="images/roasted-chickpeas.jpg" alt="Tray of deep-golden roasted chickpeas" width="250" align="right"> -->

Chickpeas roasted hot until deep golden and firm, tossed with spice. Eat warm
by the handful as a snack, scatter over salads and bowls, or fold into the
[taco-seasoned wraps](taco-seasoned-wraps.md) as a plant-protein filling.

**Tags:** cuisine: Mediterranean / Levantine · course: grazing / snack /
protein add-on · dietary: vegan, GF · make-ahead: partial (crisp fades — see
below)

## Yields & scaling

- **Base batch:** 2 × 400 g cans (~480 g drained) → a big grazing bowl for
  ~4–6, or a topping for a day's salads. From dried: ~250 g dried → ~600 g
  cooked.
- **Wrap-scale (this event):** the wraps want **1.5 kg dried, or 6 × 400 g
  cans** — that's **3× the base batch**. Match it to the wrap headcount, not
  this bowl.
- **Scaling is linear, but the real ceiling is tray area, not the recipe.**
  Crisp needs a **single, un-crowded layer** — heaped chickpeas trap steam and
  come out pale and soft instead of golden. On site that means the
  **6-tray convection oven** ([`../2026/equipment.md`](../2026/equipment.md))
  sets how much you can roast at once; do it in batches rather than piling the
  trays. Convection is a bonus here — the moving dry air crisps better than a
  still oven.

## Equipment

<!-- only the non-obvious kit -->
- **Sheet pans + baking parchment** — one loose layer per pan.
- **A clean tea towel** — for drying the chickpeas (the single biggest crisp
  factor; see step 2).

## Ingredients

_Per base batch (2 × 400 g cans / ~250 g dried). Spice column: pick **one**
direction — see [Variations](#garnishes--variations)._

| Ingredient | Amount | On the shelf as (DA · DE · FR · NL) |
|---|---|---|
| Chickpeas — canned, or dried | 2 × 400 g cans **or** ~250 g dried | kikærter · Kichererbsen · pois chiches · kikkererwten |
| Oil (olive or neutral) | 1½–2 tbsp | — |
| Salt | ~½ tsp, to taste | salt · Salz · sel · zout |
| **Smoky default:** smoked paprika + ground cumin | ~1 tsp each | røget paprika · geräuchertes Paprikapulver · paprika fumé · gerookt paprikapoeder |
| **or Taco set** (for the wraps) | ~2 units of the [wraps' taco seasoning](taco-seasoned-wraps.md#taco-seasoning-make-it-from-single-spices) | — |

> **Cumin ≠ caraway.** Danish **spidskommen** and German **Kreuzkümmel** are
> cumin; plain *kommen* / *Kümmel* is caraway — a different, aniseedy spice that
> will throw the flavour off.

> **Canned is the easy path here.** Roasting wants a firm chickpea, and canned
> ones roast fine — no soak, no simmer. Cook dried only if you're already
> cooking a big pot for the wraps' beans anyway.

## Method

<!-- One linear thread. The drying in step 2 is the whole game — spell it out. -->

1. **Get the chickpeas ready.** Canned: drain and rinse. Dried: soak 8–12 h,
   then simmer until tender but not falling apart (or cook them alongside the
   [wraps' beans](taco-seasoned-wraps.md#method)), and drain. Heat the oven to
   **200–220 °C** (fan/convection ~200 °C) — a properly hot oven is what drives
   off the water and browns them.
2. **Dry them really well — this is the step that makes or breaks it.** Tip the
   chickpeas onto a tea towel, pat the tops dry, then roll them around so the
   towel pulls off the surface water and most of the loose papery skins. Wet
   chickpeas *steam* instead of roasting and never crisp; the drier they go in,
   the better they come out. Discard the skins that shed.
3. **Oil and salt only — no ground spices yet** (why in step 5). Spread the dry
   chickpeas on a parchment-lined sheet pan, drizzle with the **oil**, add the
   **salt**, and toss to coat. Spread them into **one loose layer with space
   between them** — crowd the pan and they steam. Use a second pan rather than
   piling one.
4. **Roast until deep golden and dry, ~25–35 min**, shaking the pan (or stirring)
   once or twice so they colour evenly. They're done when they're a rich brown,
   firm, and *rattle* against the pan — not soft in the middle. Timing depends
   on how dry and how big they were, so go by look and sound, not the clock.
5. **Season at the end.** In the last ~5 minutes, or the moment they come out of
   the oven, toss the hot chickpeas with the **spice** (smoky or taco set) and a
   small extra splash of oil to help it stick. Adding ground spice *now* rather
   than before matters most for the taco blend: **garlic and onion powder scorch
   bitter** over a long, hot roast, where plain cumin and paprika would survive.
   Taste and add salt.
6. **Serve warm** — that's when they're crispest.

## Garnishes / variations

Vegan by default. Change the spice in step 5:

- **Smoky (default):** smoked paprika + cumin + salt. All-purpose.
- **Taco:** ~2 units of the [wraps' taco seasoning](taco-seasoned-wraps.md#taco-seasoning-make-it-from-single-spices)
  — the version for folding into the wraps.
- **Za'atar / Levantine:** za'atar + a squeeze of lemon off the heat.
- **Sweet-heat:** a spoon of honey (or maple, to stay vegan) + harissa or
  cayenne, tossed while hot so it glazes.
- **Salt-and-vinegar:** a splash of vinegar with the salt at the end.

## Make-ahead / cross-day notes

- **Crisp is fleeting — roast them late.** They're crispest within a couple of
  hours of the oven and **soften as they cool, in the fridge, and once baked
  inside a wrap.** There's no fix for this; it's the nature of the bean.
- **So the goal shifts by use.** As a snack or salad topping, serve them fresh
  and warm. In the wraps, don't chase shatter-crisp — you're after the **deep
  roasted, spiced flavour and a firm (not soggy) texture** that survives the
  bake, which is exactly why they get roasted rather than just stirred in.
- **Leftovers:** stir cooled ones into salads and grain bowls, blitz into a
  crunchy topping, or scatter over soup. They won't re-crisp fully, but a few
  minutes back in a hot oven revives them somewhat.
