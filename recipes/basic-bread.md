# Basic bread

House sourdough-yeast rolls, mixed in the Ankarsrum. Scales from a weeknight
batch to full event size on the same ratios.

**Tags:** cuisine: Danish/general · course: bread · dietary: vegan ·
make-ahead: no

## Yields & scaling

- **Base batch:** ~12 rolls (feeds 4–5).
- **Scaling:** linear up to 5 kg flour (~60 rolls, feeds 20–25) — this is the
  largest batch tested in the Ankarsrum bowl. At max size the first proof
  overflows the mixer bowl; move dough to a separate bowl to proof.

## Equipment

- Ankarsrum stand mixer (on-site kit)

## Ingredients

_Per base batch (serves ~4–5); max batch in parentheses (serves ~20–25)._

| Ingredient | Amount (base) | Amount (max) | On the shelf as (DA) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manitoba flour (strong bread flour) | 300 g | 1500 g | manitobamel |
| Whole wheat flour, coarse | 200 g | 1000 g | groft hvedemel |
| Water | 365 g | 1800 g | |
| Sourdough starter | 100 g | 500 g | surdej |
| Yeast | 10 g | 50 g | gær |
| Salt | 10 g | 50 g | salt |

## Method

1. Add water and yeast to the mixer bowl; run until the yeast is dissolved.
2. Base batch: add all remaining ingredients except salt in one go.
   Max batch: add the flour in batches over about a minute instead — dumping
   5 kg in at once overloads the mixer.
3. Add salt in the last 5 minutes of mixing.
4. Mix 5 minutes on low, then 10 minutes on medium–high.
5. First proof: 60–90 minutes. Max batch will overflow the mixer bowl —
   transfer to a separate bowl before proofing.
6. Shape and proof again, 60–90 minutes.
