# Brunsviger

Danish sweet yeast bread with a butter-brown-sugar topping baked straight into
the surface. Serve as a 4 o'clock treat, cut into squares.

**Tags:** cuisine: Danish · course: 4 o'clock / bread · dietary: vegetarian ·
make-ahead: no (best fresh from the oven)

## Yields & scaling

- **Base batch:** 3 gastro trays, serves 50–60.
- Recipe is sized for the full batch above; halve for one tray.

## Equipment

- Stand mixer with dough hook (large batch — see [equipment list](../2026/equipment.md))
- 3 gastro trays (hotel pans), parchment paper
- Oven, 170°C

## Ingredients

_Per base batch (3 gastro trays, serves 50–60)._

### Dough

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Yeast | 6 packets |
| Cold water | 1.5 L |
| Flour | 2.6 kg |
| Sugar | 300 g |
| Butter, soft | 300 g |
| Salt | 6 tsp |

### Topping

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Butter | 450 g |
| Brown caster sugar | 1.5 kg |
| Maple syrup | 12 tbsp |
| Cinnamon | to taste |

## Method

1. **Mix the dough:** combine all dough ingredients in the stand mixer with
   the hook attachment.
2. **First proof:** let the dough double in size, about 1 hour.
3. **Shape:** flatten the dough into the gastro trays lined with parchment
   paper. Dimple the surface with your fingertips — this is what gives
   brunsviger its texture and stops the topping pooling in one spot.
4. **Second proof:** let double again, 30–60 minutes. Dimple the surface
   again once proofed.
5. **Make the topping:** melt the butter, brown sugar, maple syrup and
   cinnamon together in a pan. Don't let it boil. Let it cool slightly before
   the next step, so it doesn't melt the proofed dough.
6. **Top and bake:** spread the topping over the dough. Bake in a preheated
   oven at 170°C for 15–17 minutes.
