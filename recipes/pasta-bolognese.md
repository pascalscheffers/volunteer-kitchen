# Pasta bolognese

A rich, tomato-and-red-wine meat ragù over spiral pasta, built for a crowd.
Mushrooms and a proper long simmer give it depth and body; a vegan pot runs
alongside off the same vegetable base so mixed diets eat the same dish.

**Tags:** cuisine: Italian(-ish) · course: dinner · dietary: meat default,
vegan pot as a swap · make-ahead: yes (sauce a day ahead)

## Yields & scaling

- **Event target:** **100 people at ~600 g plated each = ~60 kg of finished
  dish.** Composition follows the planning split: **~40 % pasta, ~30 %
  vegetable, ~30 % meat (or soya sub)** by weight. Treat those as a guide, not
  a law — the tomato, wine and stock add sauce weight on top.
- **Batch unit = one 15 L pot of sauce ≈ 25 servings.** We have **4 burners,
  each rated for a 15 L pot**, so **4 pots = the full 100**. Cook the sauce in
  those 4 pots; boil the pasta separately (see method); combine and finish in
  the **6-tray convection oven**.
- **Pasta is measured dry, sauce ingredients are the cooked dish.** 40 % of
  60 kg is ~24 kg of *cooked* pasta ≈ **~11 kg dry** (spiral roughly doubles in
  weight when boiled).
- **Scaling is linear across pots.** Each pot is self-contained — make 3 meat
  pots + 1 vegan pot, or whatever your headcount split is. The ceiling is pot
  and oven capacity, not the recipe.
- **Validate at home first.** The **serves-4 test batch** (right-hand column) is
  the recipe at the scale the source recipes were written and tested at. Cook it
  once at home before committing 15 kg of meat to it — season the base to taste
  there, then trust the multiplier.

## Equipment

- **4× large pots (~15 L)** — one per sauce batch (on the 4 burners).
- **Convection oven, 6 trays** — final combine + reheat.
- **Deep oven trays / gastronorms** — to finish pasta + sauce in.
- **Large food processor** — blitz the soffritto (onion/carrot/celery) in
  seconds instead of hand-dicing ~10 kg. We have one on site; use it.
- **Sharp knife** for the mushrooms (bring from home — the on-site knives are
  dull; see [`../2026/equipment.md`](../2026/equipment.md)).
- **Big colander** for draining pasta; **long spoons / spider**.
- **Kitchen scale.**

## Method

The plan: **make the sauce a day ahead** and chill it, so service day only needs
pasta + reheating. One pot's worth is written below; run all 4 pots in parallel
on the 4 burners.

1. **Prep the vegetables (food processor).** Peel the onion, carrot and garlic;
   trim the celery. Blitz onion, carrot, celery and garlic in the food processor
   to a fine, even rubble — not a purée. Slice the mushrooms by hand (the
   processor turns them to mush). Dice the bacon.
2. **Render the bacon.** Warm a little olive oil in the pot over medium heat, add
   the bacon, and fry until the fat has rendered and the bits are turning golden,
   ~8–10 min. That rendered fat is flavour — cook the vegetables in it.
3. **Sweat the soffritto.** Tip in the onion/carrot/celery/garlic rubble and the
   picked rosemary. Cook gently, stirring often, until soft and sweet, ~10–15
   min. **Don't rush this with high heat** — you want it soft and mellow, not
   browned and harsh. This is the flavour base of the whole sauce.
4. **Brown the mushrooms.** Push the soffritto aside, raise the heat, add a
   little more oil and the mushrooms. **Give them room and don't stir too soon** —
   mushrooms crowded in a pot dump their water and boil grey; spread out and left
   alone they brown and go savoury. Work in two goes if the pot's crowded. Salt
   them once they've coloured (salting early pulls the water out).
5. **Brown the beef.** Raise the heat, crumble in the mince, and let it sit
   before stirring so it sears rather than steams. Break up the lumps and cook
   until no longer pink and some of it has caught golden. Season with salt and
   pepper. _(Vegan pot: see the swap box — brown the rehydrated soya here
   instead.)_
6. **Deglaze with wine.** Pour in the red wine, scrape the browned bits off the
   bottom, and let it bubble down by about half — a couple of minutes. This lifts
   all the stuck-on flavour back into the sauce and cooks off the raw-alcohol
   edge.
7. **Build the sauce.** Stir in the tomato purée and cook it out 1–2 min (it
   loses its raw, tinny taste), then add the tinned tomatoes, oregano, bay, and
   enough hot **vegetable stock** to make a loose sauce. Add a **pinch of sugar**
   to balance the tomatoes' acidity.
8. **Simmer long and low.** Bring to a gentle simmer, part-cover, and cook at a
   lazy blip for **at least 1½ hours, 2–3 if you have the burner** — stirring now
   and then, topping up with a splash of stock or water if it tightens too far.
   Time is what turns this from "meat in tomato sauce" into a deep, glossy ragù.
9. **Enrich (meat pots).** In the last 20 min stir in the **milk or cream** and
   let it melt into the sauce — it rounds off the acidity and adds richness
   (the traditional Bolognese move). Finish with a **knob of butter** off the
   heat for gloss.
10. **Season and cool.** Taste hard and correct — salt, pepper, maybe a touch
    more sugar. It should taste bold; pasta mutes it. **Chill fast and hold in
    the walk-in** overnight; the flavour deepens.

### Service day — pasta + finish

11. **Boil the pasta in batches, slightly under.** Free up the burners and boil
    the spiral pasta in well-salted water, working in batches (you won't fit
    11 kg at once). Cook it **1–2 min short of done** — it finishes in the oven
    with the sauce. Drain, toss with a little oil so it doesn't clump.
12. **Combine in the trays.** In each deep oven tray, mix undercooked pasta with
    reheated sauce (roughly **40 % pasta to 60 % sauce by eye**). Loosen with a
    splash of pasta water or stock if it looks dry — it thickens in the oven.
13. **Reheat through.** Bake in the convection oven until piping hot all the way
    through, ~20–30 min. **6 trays for ~60 kg is tight** — plan on **two oven
    rounds**, holding the first hot (covered, or in cambros) while the second
    bakes. Serve straight from the trays.

> **Vegan pot swap.** Replace the bacon **and** beef with **~1.5 kg dry soya
> granulate** (the ~35 % protein shredded stuff from Netto). Rehydrate it in
> ~3.5 L hot **vegetable** stock for 10 min, drain and squeeze out excess, then
> **brown it in olive oil** at step 5 like the mince — browning is what gives it
> savoury, meaty texture instead of tasting of wet cardboard. Use **oil, not
> bacon fat**, at step 2, and **skip the milk/butter** at step 9 (a spoon of
> olive oil or a splash of oat cream enriches it instead). Everything else is
> identical. Run e.g. **3 meat pots + 1 vegan pot**, adjusted to your headcount.

## Sides

- **Cheese, on top at the table:** grated **parmesan** and torn **mozzarella** —
  let people add their own so the vegan servings stay vegan. (Note: most
  supermarket parmesan/Parmigiano is made with animal rennet, so it's vegetarian-
  but-not-vegan; keep it off the vegan pot.)
- **A sharp green salad** to cut the richness — something with acid and bite
  (leaves + a mustardy vinaigret, or fennel/rocket) does more than a soft salad.
  A rich tray of ragù needs a fresh, acidic counterweight, not another heavy dish.
- **Bread** for the plates people scrape — garlic bread or focaccia if the oven
  schedule allows.

## Make-ahead / cross-day notes

- **Sauce is fully make-ahead** — better a day later, in fact. Chill fast and
  keep in the walk-in; reheat on service day.
- **Freezes** well (sauce only, not the pasta) if a batch runs over.
- **Leftovers:** ragù is next day's lasagne, a baked-pasta filling, or a
  jacket-potato topping. It reheats better than most things you'll cook all week.
- **Don't sauce pasta you're storing** — hold pasta and sauce separately and
  combine only for the tray that's going in the oven, or stored pasta turns to
  bloated mush.

## References

and [`trusted-sources.md`](trusted-sources.md)):

- [Felicity Cloake — How to make perfect bolognese (The Guardian)](https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/wordofmouth/2010/nov/25/how-to-make-perfect-bolognese) — the traditional Emilia-Romagna benchmark: soffritto, smoked bacon, milk simmered in, minimal tomato, hours-long cook. We took the **technique** (proper soffritto, brown the meat, long simmer, milk for richness) from here.
- [BBC Good Food — The best spaghetti bolognese (Andrew Balmer)](https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/best-spaghetti-bolognese-recipe) — the tomato-and-red-wine crowd version. We took the **flavour profile** (red wine, tinned tomato + purée, rosemary/bay/oregano, stock) from here, which matches this event's brief.
- Serious Eats — J. Kenji López-Alt's slow-cooked Bolognese (their site blocks automated access, so it's named rather than linked) — corroborates the long-simmer, browned-meat, soffritto approach.

**What we changed vs the sources:** neither source uses mushrooms — those are
this event's own addition, to add umami and bulk out the meat economically for a
crowd. We use **red wine** (BBC) rather than Cloake's white, and a **tomato-
forward** sauce rather than her minimal one, because the brief asks for a rich
tomato ragù. We kept her **milk/butter enrichment** and **long simmer** for depth.
Quantities are scaled to the 15 L-pot batch unit; validate the ratios on the
serves-4 test batch before scaling.
